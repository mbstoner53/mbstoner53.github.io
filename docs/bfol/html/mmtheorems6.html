5.3  Peirce's Law.
 
5.3.1  Classical Proof.
 
Theorempeirce-P4.40 517 Peirce's Law.

Cannot be proven without the Law of Excluded Middle.

⊢ (((𝜑 → 𝜓) → 𝜑) → 𝜑)
 
5.3.2  Equivalence of Peirce's Law and Law of Excluded Middle.
 
Theoremexclmid2peirce-P4.41a 518 Peirce's Law from Law of Excluded Middle. †

The use of ndexclmid-P3.16 191 is simply replaced with a hypothesis. All the other rules used are valid in intuitionist logic.

⊢ (𝜑 ∨ ¬ 𝜑)    ⇒   ⊢ (((𝜑 → 𝜓) → 𝜑) → 𝜑)
 
Theorempeirce2exclmid-P4.41b 519 Law of Excluded Middle from Peirce's Law. †

The hypothesis is a special case of Peirce's Law.

⊢ ((((𝜑 ∨ ¬ 𝜑) → ⊥ ) → (𝜑 ∨ ¬ 𝜑)) → (𝜑 ∨ ¬ 𝜑))    ⇒   ⊢ (𝜑 ∨ ¬ 𝜑)

5.4.1.1  Rule.
